Don’t Board Me

Selection & Initial Impression

The website “Don’t Board Me” was awarded Site of the Day by Awwwards on March 11, 2024, a recognition that highlights its excellent design, user experience, and creativity. The site belongs to a home pet care company and stands out for its warm aesthetic and friendly communication, prioritizing emotional experience over complex technical effects. From the very first moment, the site conveys a sense of trust and tenderness. The composition is simple yet effective: a happy dog image, a clear message, and a clean, charming design. It doesn’t aim to impress with excessive animations but rather to create an emotional connection with the visitor through friendly doodles and illustrations. In addition, the warm color palette reinforces the idea of closeness and well-being.

Structure & Information Architecture

The information architecture is direct and logical. The menu includes sections like “Services,” “Pricing,” “About,” “Blog,” and “Contact,” plus a very clear “Book Now” button that stays visible. As you scroll, the narrative is smooth and engaging: first it explains what the company does in “Services,” and it also uses option carousels so you can switch between services. Then comes the step-by-step process “How it works”, which guides you on how to proceed. Finally, you see the testimonials and pricing.

Visual Design

The color palette is soft, featuring shades of red and pink, complemented by neutral and natural tones that enhance the pet images without being distracting. The typography is modern, legible, and consistent throughout the site. The design reinforces the identity of a friendly, professional, and empathetic brand. There are no overwhelming elements, everything feels airy and harmonious. Additionally, the illustrations are very cute and visually engaging.

Interaction & Microinteractions

The site doesn’t use 3D effects or WebGL animations, but it does include microinteractions and subtle animations: entry transitions, scroll movements, hover effects on buttons, and small details like the dog’s fur moving slightly. These gentle animations make the experience more enjoyable without distracting from the content.

Usability & Accessibility

The website is clear and intuitive. Everything is easily accessible: information, pricing, booking, and contact. The design prioritizes readability, contrast, and clean navigation. It’s also very well thought out for the average user, without overwhelming them with unnecessary or hard-to-use elements. On mobile devices, the design responds correctly, although the size of some images could slightly affect loading speed.

Technical Performance

As an award-winning site, “Don’t Board Me” meets high standards of performance and accessibility. However, since it contains many images, loading times can increase if they aren’t fully optimized. Navigation is smooth, and resources are well managed. According to the Lighthouse report (featured on Awwwards), the site scores highly in design, creativity, and UX.

Excellence Criteria

The design is outstanding, achieving a perfect balance between clean aesthetics and emotional purpose. Usability is excellent, intuitive and seamless. Creativity is moderate but effective, focusing more on empathy than on technical innovation. The content is substantial and well-structured, with concise, user-friendly text.

Conclusion

This site really shows how powerful simplicity can be when it’s combined with a strong emotional message. I especially loved the overall aesthetics, everything feels warm, balanced, and visually inviting. The subtle microinteractions add a nice touch of professionalism without making the experience heavy, and the clear hierarchy with visible calls to action makes it super easy to navigate. Still, it could improve a bit by optimizing images for faster loading on mobile, maybe tweaking contrasts.

Chipsa

Selection & Initial Impression

Chipsa design immediately establishes itself as a high-level creative studio, recognized internationally and awarded by platforms such as CSSDA, Behance, Awwwards, and FWA. Its tagline, “We evoke emotions through aesthetics, WebGL and 3D,” sets the expectation for an immersive, emotionally engaging experience. On first entering the site, the impact is striking: high-quality imagery, fluid animations, and sophisticated visuals create a sense of modernity and professionalism while inviting exploration. The design feels immersive rather than overwhelming, making users want to interact further.

Structure & Information Architecture

The site demonstrates a well-considered information architecture. The top menu includes “Cases,” “About Studio,” “Sites,” “Interfaces,” “Visuals,” and “Contacts,” providing immediate clarity about the studio’s offerings. The content is presented in a chaptered structure, with a “Choose Chapter” option on the bottom left, that allows users to navigate sections at their own pace. This approach organizes the experience logically: first showcasing work, then explaining philosophy and services, and finally presenting contact information and recognitions.

Visual Design

Chipsa design excels visually, balancing soberness and sophistication. A subtle color palette allows 3D imagery and CGI elements to remain the focus, while modern, clean typography ensures readability and complements the overall aesthetic. The use of 3D and WebGL is integral to the site’s identity, giving it a unique and recognizable style that communicates expertise in digital design rather than relying solely on conventional graphics.

Interaction & Microinteractions

The site’s interactivity enhances the experience without distracting from content. Animations and transitions are smooth and purposeful, reacting to cursor movement, scroll, and section changes. Microinteractions such as hover effects and subtle element shifts create a sense of depth and engagement, making the user feel as if they are navigating a tactile space rather than a static page. It really makes the user immerse into the webpage.

Usability & Accessibility

Despite the complexity of its visuals and 3D content, Chipsa design remains intuitive and user-friendly. Navigation is clear, the hierarchy is consistent, and users can locate information without confusion. On desktop, the experience is fluid, though heavy 3D assets may affect performance on less powerful devices or slower connections. Overall the site balances visual richness with usability.

Technical Performance

Technically, the site is ambitious, combining WebGL, CGI, and large media assets while maintaining smooth navigation. Such features can challenge loading times and metrics, however the design appears well-optimized for performance. Responsiveness seems strong, adapting across screen sizes, and though accessibility might benefit from additional refinements, the technical aspects make it possible for a great immersive experience.

Excellence Criteria

Chipsa design demonstrates excellence in multiple areas. Its design is outstanding, visually striking, cohesive, and memorable. Usability is generally strong, with minor potential friction on slower devices. Creativity is very high, combining storytelling, 3D, and interactive elements in innovative ways. The content is substantive, providing case studies, descriptions, and recognition that showcase the studio’s expertise and reinforce the narrative beyond purely visual appeal.

Conclusion

Overall, Chipsa design is an exemplar of immersive, narrative-driven web design. It illustrates how emotion, interactivity, and clarity can coexist to create a memorable user experience. Key takeaways include the importance of structured navigation, smooth microinteractions, and a cohesive visual identity, while maintaining performance and accessibility. The site serves as a benchmark for how high-quality design, storytelling, and technical expertise can work together to elevate a digital portfolio.

Purpose Talent

Selection & Initial Impression

Upon visiting Purpose Talent's website, it's immediately clear that this is a boutique consultancy with a deep commitment to connecting individuals with meaningful work. The homepage greets visitors with the phrase "Work that matters," setting a purposeful tone. The design is clean and modern, with a color palette that evokes warmth and professionalism. The site feels intentional, reflecting the company's mission to align talent with purpose-driven organizations.

Structure & Information Architecture

The website's navigation is straightforward, offering clear paths for both companies and candidates. The primary sections, Companies, Candidates, and Contact, are easily accessible, ensuring that users can quickly find the information relevant to them. Additionally, the "Stories" section provides insights into the company's journey and philosophy, further enhancing the site's transparency and user engagement. However I do find it a bit frustrating that i feel like scrolling takes too long because of the animations.

Visual Design

Purpose Talent's visual design is characterized by its minimalist yet fun approach, utilizing a soft color palette that conveys professionalism and approachability. The typography is modern and legible, with ample white space that allows content to breathe. The use of imagery is thoughtful, featuring diverse individuals that reflect the company's inclusive ethos. Overall, the design is very attractive and supports the brand's message of connecting people to meaningful work.

Interaction & Microinteractions

The website offers subtle microinteractions that enhance user experience without being distracting. Hover effects on buttons and links provide tactile feedback, guiding users through the site seamlessly. The transitions between pages are smooth, and fun, contributing to a fluid browsing experience. However, as mentioned before, I do feel like at some parts, for example when they list the characteristics they like in their partners.

Usability & Accessibility

Navigating Purpose Talent's website is intuitive. The layout is consistent, and the information is well-organized, allowing users to find what they're looking for easely. The site appears to be responsive, adapting well to different screen sizes.

Technical Performance

Purpose Talent’s website demonstrates strong technical execution while incorporating a range of animations and microanimations that enhance the user experience. Page transitions are smooth, and elements like buttons, links, and images feature subtle hover effects and motion that provide visual feedback. Scrolling triggers gentle entrance animations for content blocks, creating a dynamic flow as users navigate through sections. Despite these animations, the site maintains fast load times and responsiveness across desktop, and mobile devices, showing careful optimization of assets and code. The balance between interactivity, motion, and performance ensures that the animations feel polished, fun and purposeful.

Excellence Criteria

Purpose Talent's website excels in several areas. The design is aesthetically pleasing and aligns with the company's mission. The content is clear and compelling, effectively communicating the company's values and services. The user experience is seamless, with intuitive navigation and thoughtful interactions. Overall, the site serves as a strong digital representation of Purpose Talent's brand.

Conclusion

Purpose Talent’s website successfully communicates the company’s mission of connecting individuals with meaningful, purpose-driven work. Through a combination of clear structure, thoughtful visuals, and subtle but polished animations, the site creates a professional yet approachable atmosphere. Users can easily navigate the content, whether exploring services for companies, opportunities for candidates, or the company’s story. The use of microinteractions and smooth transitions adds a layer of engagement, making the browsing experience dynamic without overwhelming the user. Overall, the website effectively reflects the brand’s values, building trust and credibility while providing an intuitive and visually appealing experience. It serves as a strong example of how simplicity, clarity, and purposeful motion can come together to create a site that is both functional and emotionally resonant.

You Can Be Anything

Selection & Initial Impression

YouCanBeAnything by TFTL Agency, awarded Site of the Day on Awwwards, is a visually striking and conceptually ambitious web experience that reimagines the Barbie universe through design and storytelling. From the very first moment, the site feels like an art piece rather than a traditional webpage. Bold typography, cinematic visuals, and layered compositions create a sense of wonder and nostalgia. The first impression is powerful, it feels immersive, glamorous, and slightly surreal, since they are literaly pulling the viewer through a heart shape hole into an editorial-style digital world.

Structure & Information Architecture

The website is organized into thematic sections rather than a conventional navigation layout. Each part, such as “History,” “Role Models,” “Collabs,” or “The Movie”, acts as a chapter in an evolving narrative about Barbie’s cultural journey. The structure encourages scrolling exploration instead of step-by-step browsing, which reinforces the idea of storytelling over functionality. There is minimal use, or necessity, for menus, as the design relies on scroll progression and visual cues to guide users through different layers of the story. Furthermore I thinks its worth to mention they include little fun folders in which by hovering you can access some cool images that give you further information.

Visual Design

Visually, the design is bold and unapologetically expressive. It mixes glossy high-fashion photography with editorial typography and dynamic compositions. The color palette shifts fluidly from bright Barbie pinks and soft pastels to darker tones, depending on the story moment, which keeps the visual rhythm interesting. The typography is treated as a visual element itself, often oversized, layered, and in motion, creating a magazine-like aesthetic that feels both nostalgic and modern. Every section feels carefully art-directed to evoke emotion and brand legacy.

Interaction & Microinteractions

Interaction is where the site truly shines. Motion and animation are deeply embedded into the storytelling, images turn, fade and slide gracefully into view, typography reacts to scroll, and transitions feel cinematic. Hover effects subtly reveal additional layers of content, like the folders mentioned before, and scroll-triggered animations add rhythm and flow. These microinteractions bring the site to life without feeling gimmicky, reinforcing the feeling of being inside a digital exhibition. The animations are not only decorative; they carry narrative meaning. One wirth mentioning is a spiral like animation that sucks you in as you scroll.

Usability & Accessibility

While the experience is visually immersive, it prioritizes artistic expression over pure usability. The layered typography and complex backgrounds can occasionally reduce readability, and navigation lacks the conventional clarity of utility websites. However, for the site’s storytelling purpose, the experience remains engaging and coherent, especially for users accustomed to creative showcase sites. On desktop, it performs smoothly; on mobile, some animations may feel more constrained.

Technical Performance

Technically, the website performs impressively considering its complexity. The animations run fluidly, and transitions are polished, suggesting careful use of optimization. However, due to the large volume of high-resolution media and interactive elements, the initial load time may still be heavier on slower networks. Techniques like lazy loading seem to be well implemented, keeping scroll performance stable. The experience is best enjoyed on modern hardware and fast connections, where its cinematic qualities can fully shine.

Excellence Criteria

In terms of design, the project is outstanding, it’s cohesive, daring, and visually rich. Usability takes a secondary role to the creative vision, but the flow remains understandable enough for its artistic intent. Creativity is where the site excels most: it redefines what a digital campaign can look like, turning a nostalgic brand story into an immersive digital experience. The content balances cultural storytelling with strong visual identity, making the experience both emotional and memorable.

Conclusion

YouCanBeAnything is a remarkable example of how web design can transcend functionality to become pure storytelling. Winning Awwwards’ Site of the Day feels fully deserved, as the project merges design, technology, and emotion into one coherent piece. It successfully communicates the timeless message of the Barbie brand, empowerment, imagination, and identity through a contemporary and artistic digital lens. The smooth transitions, creative typography, and cinematic animations make the site feel alive and deeply intentional. While some usability and accessibility compromises are evident, they seem like conscious trade-offs in favor of expression and immersion. Overall, this project really stands out for me in terms of interactive storytelling and visual design, showing how digital experiences can be as evocative as film or print when crafted with vision and precision.
